I've been meaning to start writing for a long time.

Not the polished, SEO-optimised kind of writing where every post has a "key takeaway" box and a call to action at the bottom. Just notes. Honest ones.

## Why now?

I'm in a strange season of life. Running a few small projects, growing food on the roof, spending too much time reading about systems. Chiang Mai is a good place for this kind of slow, deliberate work — there's space here to think.

Writing feels like the right thing to do when you're in the middle of something you don't fully understand yet. It forces clarity that reading alone never gives you.

## What I'll write about

Three things take up most of my mental cycles right now:

**Entrepreneurship** — specifically the solo kind. No investors, no co-founders, no growth-at-all-costs. Just a person and a problem and the long game. I'm interested in the economics of it, the psychology of it, and the practical day-to-day decisions most people don't talk about.

**Hydroponics** — I started growing lettuce, basil, and pak choi indoors about eight months ago using a nutrient film technique system I built myself. It's been humbling. I'll write about what works, what doesn't, and why I think controlled-environment agriculture matters more than most people realise.

**Technology** — the tools I actually use, ideas I find interesting, and an honest view of what hype looks like from the ground rather than from a conference stage.

## A note on language

Most posts will be in English, but I'll occasionally write in Thai when it feels more natural for a given topic. Both are home.
